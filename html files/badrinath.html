About this Trek

Badrinath Temple, dedicated to Lord Vishnu, is a prominent Hindu pilgrimage site nestled in the Garhwal Himalayas of Uttarakhand, India, at an elevation of approximately 3,100 meters (10,170 feet).

This sacred temple is one of the Char Dham pilgrimage destinations and is believed to have been established by Adi Shankaracharya in the 8th century.

Surrounded by majestic snow-capped peaks and the Alaknanda River flowing nearby, Badrinath Temple attracts devotees and visitors seeking spiritual solace and natural beauty.
